Coffee House Scheduler

Overview:

Rice Coffeehouse is a student-run business at Rice University that employs over forty employees and serves over 4000 students and faculty. For many at Rice, Coffeehouse serves as a central hub on campus to study, meet friends, and get caffeinated. As a result, Coffeehouse must hire a lot of student employees, formally known as “Keepers of Coffee” (KOC’s, aka baristas), to meet their high demand. As a student-run business, student employees often have busy schedules during the academic year, making it difficult to schedule appropriate shifts for all employees while still meeting the required number of employees per hour to keep Coffeehouse running smoothly. As a result, a large responsibility falls upon a student manager each semester to manually schedule student employees such that it caters to the busy schedules of student employees, yet satisfy the demanding business operations of the coffee shop. We approximate that over 40+ hours is spent on the scheduling process each semester. Our team hopes to reduce this number ten-fold and eventually create an application that fully automates the entire scheduling process. This way, Coffeehouse can spend more of their time improving campus life and adding to the diverse culture at Rice University.


    Scheduling Process

  1. KOCs scheduled at the beginning of semester to work certain shifts every week.
  2. Four weeks into semester, new schedule is created to include new hires.
  3. Throughout the semester, shifts are traded over the Coffeehouse listserv.
  4. Repeat process each semester.

    Timeline (40-50+ hours):

  • A month before semester begins:
    • Manager sends out google form to all employees
    • Employees fill out google worksheet (10-15 min/employee)
      • Availability (1-4, 1: Would prefer, 2: Ambivalent, 3: Would prefer not to, 4: Cannot)
      • Additional information (min/max hours, special requests, preferences, etc)
    • Manager imports raw data into various google sheets
    • Manager begins scheduling process (10-12 hours for manager)
    • Manager sends schedule to employees via email
    • Employees submit trade request via email (2-3 hours for manager)
  • Once the semester starts:
    • Permanent and temporary trades continue, manager modifies the schedule based on these updated requests (30 min for manager)
  • 4 weeks into the semester:
    • A new schedule is required to include new hires
    • Entire scheduling process repeats itself (21 hours for manager)
  • Rest of the semester:
      Permanent and temporary trades continue, manager modifies the schedule based on these updated requests (5 hours for manager)

    Current Scheduling Steps/ Shift Prioritization:

  1. Fill Tuesday/Thursday 9-11 am slot
      Tuesday and Thursday classes are difficult to schedule around
  2. Fill Monday/Wednesday/Friday 9-11 am slot
      Employees don’t like doing morning shifts
  3. Fill closing shifts (they are low demand)
      No one likes to be on closing shifts
  4. Fill 1-5pm slots for all days
      Busiest time of the day
  5. Fill out the first of the remaining slots

    Current Process of Trading Shifts:

  • Email is sent out to all employees showing the schedule
  • Employees can request to temporarily or permanently trade shifts with other employees via email if they have a conflicting shift
  • Employees will negotiate via email with other employees and see if they can trade shifts
  • Employees notify manager to receive approval and then, fill out a form (form necessary for splitting tips monthly)
  • Manager goes through data received from forms and manually calculates the change in the net work hours for the employees who traded
  • In the case of a temporary change, the schedule itself is not changed.

    Number of employees per shift

  • The number of employees per shift depends on Coffeehouse’s business during the specified time slot
  • Example: shifts 1pm-5pm would require 4-5 people whereas shifts 5pm-7pm would require 3 people
  • Opening / Closing: 2 required
  • 8 - 9am: 2 required
  • 9am - 1pm: 3 required
  • 1 - 5pm: 5 required
  • 5 - 7pm: 3 required
  • 7 - 11pm: 3 required


Part 1: Scoping

Problem: The current Rice Coffeehouse shift scheduling and trading system is time-consuming, unintuitive, and prone to human error.

  • Shift scheduling data is gathered through a Google Survey sent out to each employee and managed using several Google Excel worksheets.
  • The Coffeehouse scheduling manager must navigate between these worksheets to scan for information when creating the semester’s schedule.
  • Problems with managing data in Google worksheets:
    • Data visualization:
      • It is tedious to scan through multiple dense spreadsheets
      • The Semester’s schedule cannot be displayed in a clean, elegant format
      • Employees are unable to see their personal schedules in a calendar view
    • Error prone:
      • Conditional formatting means 1 single error can make data unusable
      • Difficult to check for mistakes when creating the schedule e.g. A misspelled name leads to untracked hours for an employee
    • Time consuming:
      • New incoming Coffeehouse managers invest too much time to learn the complex scheduling process
  • Our solution to the problem rests on finding a way to represent this data. Questions to answer:
    • What is the best way to represent all this data efficiently?
    • How do we keep track of all the metrics required?
    • How do we prioritize data so that the scheduling manager can do their job efficiently?
  • What alternatives are out there?:
    • Sling: Manual shift scheduler
      • Con/Problem: cannot implement rules and constraints specific to scheduling chaus managers, $$$$ subscription plan, no automation
      • Pros: Good set of features for team communications and syncing with email and calendars
    • Humanity
      • Con/Problem: cannot implement rules and constraints specific to CoffeHouse
      • Pros: same as Sling
    • General problem with alternatives: These alternatives are not tailored for the specific needs of chaus.
    • So do we NEED this product that you are building? YES!

Who are we building this for?

  • Coffee House Employees
    • Employees only interact with spreadsheets when:
      • They have a shift conflict
      • They need to trade shifts with another employee
      • They set up their schedule for the semester
    • Need:
      • A quick way to send availability information to Chaus management
      • An easy way to view their scheduled shifts
  • Coffee House Manager:
    • Requires this product more than employees since they are in charge of shift scheduling
    • Needs:
        A quick way to create a schedule for employees.

What is the most important thing for our users?

  • Most important: Correctness ⇒ The schedule needs to adhere to rules/guidelines such as:
    • Each employee cannot be scheduled over their max # of hours
    • Each shift must have a minimal number of employees scheduled
    • An employee ideally should not work two separate shifts in one day
  • Second most important: Ease of use ⇒ our UI design and flow needs to be user-friendly and flexible for managers to schedule shifts as they desire

Part 2: Product Specification

Ranked List of Features:

  1. Employee Availability Calendar
    1. Ability to select available times
      • Toggle between preferred, available, not preferred, and unavailable
      • Calendar button colors reflect availability
    2. Ability to save times to backend
    3. Ability to view personal schedule
    4. Ability to request trades for a given time-slot
        Successful trades will automatically
  2. Master Scheduling Calendar
    1. Ability to schedule users for a given time-slot
      • Ability to add/remove users from ‘scheduled’ array for a given time-slot
      • Visual warnings for attempting to overschedule employees or schedule for two non-consecutive shifts in a day
    2. Ability to view additional information on individual employees or properties of timeslots
      • Employees: Hours worked so far; whether they’re already scheduled for the day
      • Timeslot Properties: Required number of employees
    3. Ability for employees to see read-only view of master schedule
  3. Login Authorization
    1. Ability to register as a manager or employee
    2. Ability to login
      • Then be redirected to employee schedule (if employee) / master schedule (if manager)
    3. Login should redirect to IDP (Rice NetID login system), get a token, store a token in our database upon successful NetID Login
  4. Create/Delete Users
    1. CRUD Operations implemented in backend
    2. Create - register user
    3. Read - login user
    4. Update - change user properties
    5. Delete - Remove user and all references to user from database
  5. Automation
    • Utilizing shift-scheduling algorithm to optimally schedule employees

List of Features for MVP

  1. Employee Availability Calendar
    1. Week long schedule viewable by both the manager and employees.
    2. Color-coded to convey how “full” each shift is
  2. Master Schedule:
    1. Week long schedule only viewable/editable by manager
    2. When shift is clicked, unscheduled/scheduled employees will be displayed
      • If unscheduled, 1 - 4 will be shown to convey levels of that employee’s availability
  3. Warning System
    1. Alert if manager tries to overbook an employee (more than once a day or more than their hour cap)
  4. TradingSystem
    1. Form that takes employees that wish to trade shifts and their hour changes as input and updates their respective hour counts in the backend
  5. Total Hour Count
    1. Each employees hours will be kept track of in the backend
  6. Elastic Search
    1. More robust and efficient way to search and process data
  7. Biweekly Reset
    1. Every other Friday at 5 pm, employee hours are reset
    2. Schedule updated to show new employee availabilities

    The MVP:

    1. Week long schedule that can be viewed by both the employees and the manger that display how full each shift is. Colors represent how full each shift is.
    2. Manager will have the ability to edit the master schedule. They will see a list of available/already booked employees when they click on a particular shift and can add/remove employees.
    3. Managers will receive a warning if they attempt to schedule an employee that is listed as unavailable for that specific slot or if they attempt to book an employee for more than one consecutiveshift for one day.
    4. A trading mechanism will allow employees to exchange shifts. This will be a form that will automatically process the trade in the backend, updating the employees' availability and their total hours booked for that week.
    5. The backend of the application will keep track of the total number of hours each employee has worked.
    6. Elastic Search will be implemented to our databases in order to allow for a more robust and efficient way to look through and process data.
    7. Friday at 5pm, the hours for each employee in the backend will reset, and the display will change to show the next weeks employee availabilities.
